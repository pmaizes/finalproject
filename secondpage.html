Impact of Social Isolation


The difference between the mortality rates of those who were isolated compared to those who socially integretated themseleves is astounishing. In a meta-analytic review discussing "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk" conducted by Julianne Holt-Lunstad, Timothy B. Smith, and J. Bradley Layton, it was confirmed that individuals who have more developed social relationships, have a 50% higher chance of increasing their longevity compared to the indivuals who socially isolate and do not maintain healthy personal and social relationships.

This study followed indivuals for about seven years and looked at varying factors: such as pre-existing health conditions, smoking and exercise. One risk factor of deceased mortality rates, that is even higher than smoking and other health factors, is social isolation. Isolation can trigger mental illness and has been proven to shorten the life span of an individual.


“Social relationships, or the relative lack thereof, constitute a major risk factor for health—rivaling the effect of well established health risk factors such as cigarette smoking, blood pressure, blood lipids, obesity and physical activity”

— House, Landis, and Umberson; Science 1988 [1]




Harlow's Monkeys


According to the Attachment Theory, proven by Harlow's Monkeys, and seen on Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs: belongingness and love needs - like social interaction - are more important towards the development of a person, than the physiologicial needs of a person. For example, the subject will usually choose intimacy, a sense of connection, and family over water, food, and shelter.



"Everyday feels like deja vu. I stopped being able to tell what day of the week it was... The not being able to be with other people made me feel lonely and isolated"

- Abigail Bauserman




Outcome of Harlow's Experiment


"Harlow's experiments offered irrefutable proof that love is vital for normal childhood development. Additional experiments by Harlow revealed the long-term devastation caused by deprivation, leading to profound psychological and emotional distress and even death." (Kendra Cherry, 2020)


It is proven that social interaction and contact, benefits the recepient in the future. If they are depreived of this basic need, than that person, or animal, will develop "social deficits" and can become isolated.
